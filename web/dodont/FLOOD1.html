DO'S AND DONTS ON FLOOD

Before a Flood

Do's:

  1. Know Your Risk: Find out if you live in a flood-prone area. Check flood maps and local weather alerts.
  2. Create an Emergency Plan: Develop a family emergency plan. Include evacuation routes and communication plans.
  3. Prepare an Emergency Kit: Include essentials such as water, non-perishable food, medications, flashlights, batteries, first aid supplies, important documents, and cash.
  4. Purchase Flood Insurance: Consider getting flood insurance if you live in a high-risk area.
  5. Secure Your Home: Elevate electrical appliances and utilities, install sump pumps, and seal walls in basements with waterproofing compounds.
  6. Keep Important Documents Safe: Store important documents in a waterproof container or digitally on a cloud service.
  7. Stay Informed: Monitor weather reports and sign up for local alerts to stay updated on flood conditions.
  8. Plan for Pets: Make sure you have a plan for your pets in case you need to evacuate.

Don’ts:

  1. Ignore Flood Risks: Don't assume you are safe from floods if you haven’t experienced one before.
  2. Neglect Maintenance: Don’t neglect regular maintenance and checks of your home’s drainage systems and gutters.
  3. Rely Solely on Homeowners Insurance: Standard homeowners insurance does not typically cover flood damage; don't overlook the need for separate flood insurance.
  4. Forget to Back Up Data: Don’t forget to back up important digital documents and family photos in case they are lost in a flood.

During a Flood

Do's:

  1. Stay Informed: Continuously monitor weather reports and emergency alerts.
  2. Move to Higher Ground: If there is a flood warning, move to higher ground immediately.
  3. Evacuate if Advised: Follow evacuation orders from local authorities without delay.
  4. Turn Off Utilities: If time permits, turn off utilities such as gas, electricity, and water to prevent damage.
  5. Avoid Floodwaters: Stay away from floodwaters, which may be contaminated or electrically charged.
  6. Wear Protective Gear: Wear sturdy shoes, gloves, and other protective clothing if you need to walk through flood-affected areas.
  7. Keep Your Phone Charged: Ensure your phone is charged for communication and emergency updates.

Don’ts:

  1. Don’t Walk or Drive Through Floodwaters: Just six inches of moving water can knock you down, and one foot of water can sweep your vehicle away.
  2. Don’t Ignore Evacuation Orders: Don’t stay in your home if authorities advise you to evacuate.
  3. Avoid Using Electrical Appliances: Don’t use electrical appliances that have been in contact with water.
  4. Don’t Touch Electrical Equipment: Avoid touching electrical equipment if you are wet or standing in water.

After a Flood

Do's:

  1. Wait for Official Declaration: Return home only when authorities have declared it safe.
  2. Check for Structural Damage: Inspect your home for structural damage before entering.
  3. Document Damage: Take photos and videos of the damage for insurance claims.
  4. Wear Protective Clothing: Wear rubber boots, gloves, and masks when cleaning up to protect from contaminants.
  5. Remove Water Safely: Pump out flooded basements gradually (about one-third of the water per day) to avoid structural damage.
  6. Clean and Disinfect: Clean and disinfect everything that got wet to prevent mold growth and contamination.
  7. Boil Water: Boil drinking water until authorities declare the water supply safe.

Don’ts:

  1. Don’t Ignore Health Hazards: Don’t ignore signs of mold growth or contamination; they can cause serious health issues.
  2. Don’t Use Flooded Appliances: Don’t use electrical appliances that have been in contact with water until they have been inspected and declared safe.
  3. Avoid Flood-Damaged Roads: Don’t drive on roads that have been damaged or covered by floodwaters.
  4. Don’t Dispose of Flood Debris Improperly: Follow local guidelines for disposing of flood-damaged items and debris.
