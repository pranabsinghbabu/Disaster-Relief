DO'S AND DONTS ON COLD-WAVE

Do's:

  1. Stay informed: Keep updated with weather forecasts and warnings from reliable sources like the National Weather Service or local meteorological departments to know when a cold wave is expected and how severe it might be.
  2. Dress warmly: Wear multiple layers of loose-fitting, lightweight, warm clothing rather than one heavy layer. Make sure to cover extremities with hats, gloves, scarves, and thermal socks. Waterproof and windproof outer layers are ideal.
  3. Insulate your home: Ensure that your home is well-insulated. Use weather stripping around doors and windows to prevent drafts. Close curtains or blinds to retain heat.
  4. Maintain heating equipment: Ensure your heating equipment is in good working condition. Have your furnace inspected and cleaned. If using space heaters, ensure they are safe and placed away from flammable materials.
  5. Stock emergency supplies: Keep a supply of non-perishable food, water, medications, batteries, flashlights, and a first aid kit in case of power outages or emergencies.
  6. Stay indoors: Limit time spent outdoors during extreme cold. If you must go outside, stay as dry as possible since wet clothing chills the body rapidly.
  7. Heat your home safely: Use fireplaces, wood stoves, or space heaters properly. Ensure adequate ventilation to avoid carbon monoxide poisoning. Keep a fire extinguisher nearby and know how to use it.
  8. Stay hydrated: Drink plenty of fluids to avoid dehydration. Warm beverages can help maintain body temperature.
  9. Check on vulnerable individuals: Regularly check on elderly neighbors, young children, and people with disabilities or chronic illnesses who may need help during extreme cold.
  10. Prepare your vehicle: If you need to travel, make sure your vehicle is winterized with antifreeze, a full tank of gas, and an emergency kit including blankets, food, water, and a shovel.

Don'ts:

  1. Don't ignore weather warnings: Take cold wave warnings seriously and follow advice from local authorities.
  2. Don't overexert yourself: Avoid strenuous activities like shoveling snow or walking in deep snow, which can strain your heart. Take breaks and work slowly.
  3. Don't use unsafe heating methods: Avoid using ovens, charcoal grills, or portable gasoline heaters indoors as they can produce dangerous carbon monoxide fumes.
  4. Don't leave pets outside: Bring pets indoors or ensure they have adequate warm shelter and access to unfrozen water.
  5. Don't neglect your pipes: Let faucets drip a little to avoid freezing pipes. Know how to shut off water valves in case of burst pipes.
  6. Don't leave your car running in an enclosed space: Never run your car inside a garage or other enclosed space, as this can lead to carbon monoxide poisoning.
  7. Don't venture out unprepared: If you must go outside, dress in appropriate clothing and limit the time spent in the cold. Carry a fully charged cell phone in case of emergencies.
  8. Don't forget to check smoke and carbon monoxide detectors: Ensure that all smoke and carbon monoxide detectors are working correctly to provide early warnings in case of fires or gas leaks.
  9. Don't consume alcohol: Avoid drinking alcohol, as it can cause your body to lose heat more rapidly and impair your judgment regarding exposure to the cold.
  10. Don't ignore symptoms of cold-related illnesses: Be aware of the signs of hypothermia (shivering, exhaustion, confusion, fumbling hands, memory loss, slurred speech) and frostbite (numbness, white or grayish-yellow skin, firm or waxy skin). Seek medical attention immediately if these symptoms occur.
