DO'S AND DONTS ON BLIZZARD

Do's:

  1. Stay indoors: If possible, remain indoors during a blizzard to avoid exposure to extreme cold and hazardous conditions.
  2. Keep informed: Stay updated on weather forecasts and warnings through local news, radio, or weather apps to know when a blizzard is expected and how long it might last.
  3. Prepare an emergency kit: Stock up on essential supplies such as non-perishable food, water, medications, batteries, flashlights, blankets, and a first aid kit in case of power outages or being snowed in.
  4. Dress warmly: Wear multiple layers of clothing, including a hat, gloves, scarf, and waterproof boots, to stay warm if you need to go outside or in case heating fails indoors.
  5. Insulate your home: Seal any drafts, close curtains or blinds to trap heat, and use draft stoppers at doors to prevent cold air from entering your home.
  6. Have a backup heating source: If possible, have an alternative heating source such as a fireplace, wood stove, or space heater available, but ensure it is used safely and ventilated properly to prevent carbon monoxide poisoning.
  7. Stay hydrated: Drink plenty of fluids, including warm beverages, to stay hydrated during the cold weather.
  8. Charge electronic devices: Keep your cell phone, laptop, and other electronic devices fully charged in case of power outages.
  9. Stay in touch: Keep in touch with family, friends, and neighbors, especially those who may need assistance during the blizzard.
  10. Be prepared for emergencies: Have a plan in place for emergencies, including how to evacuate if necessary, and keep important documents and emergency contacts easily accessible.

Don'ts:

  1. Don't travel unless absolutely necessary: Avoid driving or walking outside during a blizzard unless it is an emergency. Visibility can be severely reduced, and roads may be icy or impassable.
  2. Don't ignore official warnings: Take blizzard warnings seriously and follow the advice of local authorities and emergency services.
  3. Don't overexert yourself: Shoveling heavy snow or trying to clear blocked pathways can be physically demanding. Take breaks, pace yourself, and avoid overexertion to prevent injury or exhaustion.
  4. Don't use outdoor heating sources indoors: Never use portable generators, charcoal grills, camp stoves, or other fuel-burning devices indoors, as they can produce deadly carbon monoxide gas.
  5. Don't leave pets outside: Bring pets indoors or provide them with adequate shelter and protection from the cold during a blizzard.
  6. Don't leave fires unattended: If using a fireplace or wood stove for heat, never leave it unattended, and ensure that ashes are disposed of safely.
  7. Don't ignore signs of frostbite or hypothermia: Be aware of symptoms such as numbness, tingling, or discolored skin, and seek medical attention immediately if you or someone else shows signs of cold-related injuries.
  8. Don't rely solely on technology: While electronic devices can be helpful for communication and staying informed, they may not always be available during a power outage. Have alternative methods of communication and information available, such as a battery-powered radio.
  9. Don't forget to check on vulnerable individuals: Keep an eye out for elderly neighbors, young children, and individuals with disabilities who may need assistance during extreme weather conditions.
  10. Don't panic: Stay calm and follow your emergency plan if conditions worsen. Remaining calm can help you make rational decisions and stay safe during a blizzard.
