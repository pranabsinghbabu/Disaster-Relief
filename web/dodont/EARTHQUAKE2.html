DO'S AND DONTS ON EARTHQUAKE

After an Earthquake

Do's:

  1. Check for Injuries: Attend to your injuries first, then assist others if possible. Use first aid supplies if needed.
  2. Inspect for Hazards: Look out for hazards such as gas leaks, damaged electrical lines, or structural damage.
  3. Use Phones for Emergencies Only: Keep phone lines clear for emergency communications.
  4. Follow Emergency Instructions: Listen to emergency broadcasts and follow instructions from authorities.
  5. Be Prepared for Aftershocks: Aftershocks can occur; be ready to Drop, Cover, and Hold On again.
  6. Check Utilities: If you smell gas or suspect a leak, turn off the gas supply and report it to authorities. Do not use electrical devices if you suspect a gas leak.

Don’ts:

  1. Don’t Use Open Flames: Avoid using candles, matches, or lighters until you are sure there are no gas leaks.
  2. Don’t Enter Damaged Buildings: Stay away from damaged structures until they have been inspected and deemed safe.
  3. Don’t Turn on Electrical Appliances: Avoid turning on electrical appliances until you are sure there are no gas leaks or electrical damage.
  4. Don’t Drink Tap Water: Avoid drinking tap water until it has been declared safe by authorities.
  5. Avoid Driving: Do not drive unless absolutely necessary to keep roads clear for emergency vehicles.

Before an Earthquake

Do's:

  1. Create an Emergency Plan: Develop a family emergency plan and make sure all family members know what to do during an earthquake.
  2. Prepare an Emergency Kit: Include essentials like water, non-perishable food, flashlight, batteries, first aid supplies, medications, and important documents.
  3. Secure Heavy Items: Anchor heavy furniture, appliances, and other large items to walls. Use straps and brackets to prevent them from falling.
  4. Know Safe Spots: Identify safe spots in each room, such as under sturdy furniture, against an interior wall, and away from windows.
  5. Conduct Drills: Practice earthquake drills regularly to ensure everyone knows how to react.
  6. Learn First Aid: Basic first aid knowledge can be crucial in the aftermath of an earthquake.
  7. Inspect Your Home: Check the structure of your home and retrofit if necessary, especially if you live in an older building.

Don’ts:

  1. Ignore Risks: Don’t assume an earthquake won’t happen; prepare regardless of perceived risk.
  2. Leave Heavy Items Unsecured: Avoid leaving heavy objects on high shelves where they can fall and cause injury.
  3. Forget Emergency Contacts: Don’t neglect to have a list of emergency contacts readily available.
  4. Ignore Your Building’s Vulnerabilities: Don’t ignore signs of structural weakness or fail to make necessary reinforcements.

During an Earthquake

Do's:

  1. Drop, Cover, and Hold On: Drop to your hands and knees, take cover under sturdy furniture, and hold on until the shaking stops.
  2. Stay Indoors: If you are inside, stay inside. Move to a safe spot like under a table.
  3. Protect Your Head and Neck: Use your arms to shield your head and neck from falling debris.
  4. Move Away from Windows: Get away from glass, windows, outside doors, and walls.
  5. Stay in Bed if You Are There: If you are in bed, stay there. Protect your head with a pillow.
  6. Use the Stairs: If you need to leave a building, use the stairs, not the elevator.

Don’ts:

  1. Don’t Run Outside: It is safer to stay inside and find cover than to run outside during the shaking.
  2. Avoid Doorways: Contrary to popular belief, doorways are not the safest place. They do not provide protection from flying or falling objects.
  3. Don’t Stand Under Heavy Fixtures: Avoid standing under heavy light fixtures, ceiling fans, or anything that might fall.
