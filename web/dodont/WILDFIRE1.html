DO'S AND DONTS ON WILD-FIRE

Before a Wildfire

Do's:

  1. Know Your Risk: Determine if you live in a wildfire-prone area by consulting local fire departments or online resources.
  2. Create an Emergency Plan: Develop a family emergency plan that includes evacuation routes, a meeting point, and communication plans.
  3. Prepare an Emergency Kit: Include essentials such as water, non-perishable food, medications, flashlights, batteries, first aid supplies, important documents, and cash.
  4. Maintain Defensible Space: Clear a defensible space around your home by removing dead vegetation, trimming trees, and clearing leaves and debris from gutters.
  5. Use Fire-Resistant Materials: Use fire-resistant materials for roofing, siding, and decks to reduce the risk of fire spreading to your home.
  6. Install Smoke Alarms and Fire Extinguishers: Ensure smoke alarms are installed and functional in your home. Have fire extinguishers readily available.
  7. Sign Up for Alerts: Register for local emergency notifications and alerts to stay informed about wildfire risks and evacuations.
  8. Create a Communication Plan: Make sure all family members know how to contact each other in case of separation.

    9. Don’ts:

    1. Ignore Fire Risks: Don’t assume you are safe from wildfires if you haven’t experienced one recently.
    2. Neglect Home Maintenance: Don’t ignore the buildup of dry leaves, dead vegetation, and other flammable materials around your property.
    3. Use Inappropriate Materials: Avoid using combustible materials for landscaping near your home.
    4. Forget About Pets: Don’t forget to include your pets in your emergency planning and evacuation plan.

    During a Wildfire

    Do's:

    1. Stay Informed: Continuously monitor local news, weather reports, and emergency alerts for updates on the wildfire.
    2. Prepare for Evacuation: Pack your emergency kit and important documents. Keep your vehicle fueled and ready to go.
    3. Evacuate Early: If evacuation orders are issued, leave immediately. Don’t wait until the last minute.
    4. Protect Your Home: Close all windows and doors to prevent embers from entering. Move flammable furniture away from windows.
    5. Wear Protective Clothing: Wear long sleeves, long pants, sturdy shoes, gloves, and a mask to protect yourself from smoke and heat.
    6. Stay Low: If you are caught in a wildfire, stay low to the ground to avoid smoke inhalation.
    7. Follow Official Instructions: Listen to local authorities and follow their instructions regarding evacuation routes and safety measures.

    Don’ts:

    1. Don’t Ignore Evacuation Orders: Don’t stay behind if authorities have issued an evacuation order.
    2. Avoid Using Flammable Items: Don’t use items such as gas-powered equipment or grills that could ignite nearby flammable materials.
    3. Don’t Block Access: Keep driveways and access roads clear for emergency vehicles and firefighters.
    4. Avoid Outdoor Activities: Don’t engage in outdoor activities that could ignite a fire, such as campfires or using fireworks.

    After a Wildfire

    Do's:

    1. Wait for Official Clearance: Return to your home only when authorities have declared it safe.
    2. Check for Hot Spots: Be cautious of smoldering debris and hot spots that may reignite.
    3. Inspect Your Home: Check for structural damage, gas leaks, and electrical hazards before entering your home.
    4. Document Damage: Take photos and videos of the damage for insurance purposes.
    5. Clean Safely: Wear protective clothing and a mask while cleaning up debris. Wet down debris to minimize dust.
    6. Dispose of Debris Properly: Follow local guidelines for disposing of fire-damaged items and debris.
    7. Seek Support: Contact local relief agencies for assistance with recovery and rebuilding efforts.

    Don’ts:

    1. Don’t Enter Unsafe Areas: Avoid entering areas that have been heavily damaged by fire until they have been inspected and deemed safe.
    2. Avoid Using Water from Unsafe Sources: Don’t use water from wells or municipal sources until it has been tested and declared safe.
    3. Don’t Touch Downed Power Lines: Stay away from downed power lines and report them to authorities immediately.
    4. Avoid Using Contaminated Items: Don’t use food, beverages, or medications that have been exposed to heat, smoke, or soot.
