DO'S AND DONTS ON VOLCANO

Before a Volcanic Eruption

Do's:

  1. Stay Informed:
    • Regularly monitor local news, weather reports, and emergency alerts for updates on volcanic activity.
    • Sign up for community alert systems.
  2. Create an Emergency Plan:
    • Develop and practice an evacuation plan with your family. Know the evacuation routes and have a designated meeting place.
    • Ensure all family members know the plan and practice it regularly.
  3. Prepare an Emergency Kit:
    • Pack essentials such as water, non-perishable food, medications, flashlights, batteries, first aid supplies, important documents, cash, and protective gear like masks and goggles.
  4. Know Your Community’s Warning System:
    • Understand the alert levels and warning signals used in your area to indicate volcanic activity.
  5. Protect Your Home:
    • Seal windows and doors to prevent ash from entering. Store outdoor furniture and equipment indoors.
    • Clear gutters, downspouts, and storm drains of ash.
  6. Have a Communication Plan:
    • Plan how to communicate with family members if you get separated.

Don’ts:

  1. Don’t Ignore Warning Signs:
    • Pay attention to official warnings and natural signs of volcanic activity, such as rumbling or sudden changes in water levels.
  2. Don’t Stay Near the Volcano:
    • Avoid hiking or camping near a volcano if there are signs of increased activity.
  3. Don’t Neglect Preparation:
    • Don’t neglect to assemble an emergency kit and make a family emergency plan.
  4. Don’t Rely Solely on Electronic Alerts:
    • Power outages can occur; have a battery-operated radio to receive updates.

During a Volcanic Eruption

Do's:

  1. Evacuate Early:
    • If evacuation orders are issued, leave immediately. Don’t wait until the last minute.
    • Follow the recommended evacuation routes and do not take shortcuts.
  2. Stay Indoors if Not Evacuating:
    • If you cannot evacuate, stay indoors with windows and doors closed to avoid ash and toxic gases.
    • Seal all openings to prevent ash from entering.
  3. Protect Your Breathing:
    • Wear masks and goggles to protect yourself from ash inhalation and eye irritation.
    • Use a damp cloth to cover your mouth and nose if you do not have a mask.
  4. Stay Informed:
    • Continuously monitor local news, weather reports, and emergency alerts for updates on the volcanic eruption.
    • Use a battery-powered radio if necessary.
  5. Be Cautious Outdoors:
    • If you must go outside, wear long sleeves, long pants, and sturdy shoes to protect your skin from ash.
    • Drive slowly if you must travel to avoid stirring up ash and to maintain visibility.
  6. Help Neighbors:
    • Assist neighbors, especially those who are elderly, disabled, or have young children, in evacuating.

Don’ts:

  1. Don’t Stay Close to the Volcano:
    • Avoid areas downwind and downstream of the volcano.
    • Stay away from river valleys and low-lying areas prone to mudflows and lahars.
  2. Don’t Use Electronic Devices Unnecessarily:
    • Conserve battery power on phones and other devices in case of power outages.
  3. Avoid Breathing in Ash:
    • Do not stay outside longer than necessary, and avoid heavy exertion.
  4. Don’t Drive on Ash-Covered Roads:
    • Avoid driving if possible. Ash can damage your vehicle and reduce visibility.
  5. Don’t Use Tap Water if Contaminated:
    • Use stored water and avoid using tap water if there are reports of contamination.

After a Volcanic Eruption

Do's:

  1. Wait for Official Clearance:
    • Return to your home only when authorities have declared it safe.
  2. Check for Injuries:
    • Administer first aid if necessary and seek medical help for serious injuries.
  3. Inspect Your Home:
    • Check your home for structural damage, gas leaks, and electrical hazards before entering.
    • Use a flashlight to inspect your home, not matches or candles.
  4. Clean Up Safely:
    • Wear protective clothing, including long sleeves, long pants, gloves, and a mask, while cleaning up ash.
    • Dampen ash before sweeping or shoveling to minimize airborne particles.
  5. Document Damage:
    • Take photos and videos of the damage for insurance claims.
  6. Stay Informed:
    • Continue to monitor local news for updates on relief efforts and safety information.
  7. Check Utilities:
    • Report downed power lines and gas leaks to authorities immediately.
    • Avoid using tap water until authorities confirm it is safe.

Don’ts:

  1. Don’t Enter Unsafe Areas:
    • Avoid entering areas that have not been declared safe by authorities.
  2. Don’t Touch Downed Power Lines:
    • Stay away from downed power lines and report them to authorities immediately.
  3. Avoid Driving in Ash:
    • Drive only when necessary, as ash can be harmful to your vehicle.

    • Avoid using electrical appliances or turning on lights in areas with standing water.
