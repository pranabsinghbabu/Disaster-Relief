DO'S AND DONTS ON TSUNAMI

Before a Tsunami

Do's:

  1. Know Your Risk:
    • Identify if you live in a tsunami-prone area by consulting local hazard maps and resources.
    • Be aware of the history of tsunamis in your area.
  2. Create an Emergency Plan:
    • Develop a family emergency plan, including evacuation routes, a communication plan, and a designated meeting place.
    • Practice the plan with your family.
  3. Prepare an Emergency Kit:
    • Include essentials such as water, non-perishable food, medications, flashlights, batteries, first aid supplies, important documents, and cash.
    • Ensure the kit is easily accessible.
  4. Know the Signs of a Tsunami:
    • Be aware of natural signs such as a strong earthquake, a rapid rise or fall in coastal waters, or a loud ocean roar.
  5. Stay Informed:
    • Sign up for local emergency alerts and monitor weather forecasts regularly.
    • Have a battery-powered NOAA weather radio for updates in case of power outages.
  6. Plan for Pets:
    • Include pets in your emergency planning and ensure you have supplies for them.

Don’ts:

  • Ignore Natural Warnings:
    • Don’t disregard natural signs of a tsunami, such as unusual ocean behavior.
  • Delay Evacuation:
    • Don’t wait for official warnings if you notice the natural signs of a tsunami.
    • Neglect Home Preparation:
    • Don’t forget to secure heavy items in your home that could become hazards during an earthquake or tsunami.
    Forget Important Documents:
    • Don’t forget to include important documents and identification in your emergency kit.

    During a Tsunami

    Do's:

    1. Evacuate Immediately:
      • If you are in a tsunami hazard zone and feel a strong earthquake, evacuate immediately to higher ground or inland.
      • Follow established evacuation routes and go as far inland as possible.
    2. Stay Informed:
      • Listen to local authorities and follow their instructions.
      • Use a battery-powered radio to stay updated on the situation.
    3. Help Others:
      • Assist neighbors, especially those who are elderly, disabled, or have young children, in evacuating.
    4. Move to Higher Ground:
      • Move to higher ground or as far inland as possible. Even small tsunamis can be dangerous.
    5. Stay Away from the Shore:
        6. Stay away from the coast and do not go to the beach to watch the waves.
  • Don’ts:
      • Don’t Return Too Soon:
      • Don’t return to the shore until authorities have declared it safe. Multiple waves can occur hours apart.
      • Avoid Bridges:
      • Don’t use bridges to evacuate as they may be damaged or unsafe.
    1. Don’t Use Coastal Roads:
      • Avoid using coastal roads for evacuation, as they may be crowded or impassable.
      Don’t Touch Downed Power Lines:
      • Stay away from downed power lines and report them to authorities immediately.

    After a Tsunami

    Do's:

    1. Wait for Official Clearance:
      • Return to your home only when authorities have declared it safe.
    2. Check for Injuries:
      • Administer first aid if necessary and seek medical help for serious injuries.
    3. Inspect Your Home:
      • Check your home for structural damage, gas leaks, and electrical hazards before entering.
      • Use a flashlight to inspect your home, not matches or candles.
      • Document Damage:
        • Take photos and videos of the damage for insurance claims.
      • Stay Informed:
        • Continue to monitor local news for updates on relief efforts and safety information.
      • Clean Safely:
        • Wear protective clothing and masks while cleaning up debris.
        • Use proper safety gear when handling hazardous materials.
      • Check Utilities:
        • Report downed power lines and gas leaks to authorities immediately.
        • Avoid using tap water until authorities confirm it is safe.

    Don’ts:

    1. Don’t Enter Floodwaters:
      • Avoid walking or driving through floodwaters, which can be contaminated or hide dangers.
    2. Don’t Use Contaminated Food or Water:
      • Discard any food, beverages, or medications that have been exposed to floodwaters or not stored properly.
    3. Avoid Using Electricity in Wet Areas:
      • Don’t use electrical appliances or turn on lights in areas with standing water.
    4. Don’t Overexert Yourself:
      • Avoid overexertion during the cleanup process; take breaks and stay hydrated.
