DO'S AND DONTS ON TORNADO

Before a Tornado

Do's:

  1. Know Your Risk: Understand the tornado risk in your area. Familiarize yourself with the signs of an approaching tornado and the difference between a tornado watch and a tornado warning.
  2. Create an Emergency Plan: Develop a family emergency plan that includes identifying safe rooms or storm shelters, knowing how to turn off utilities, and having a communication plan.
  3. Prepare an Emergency Kit: Include essentials such as water, non-perishable food, medications, flashlights, batteries, first aid supplies, important documents, and cash.
  4. Stay Informed: Sign up for local weather alerts and monitor weather forecasts regularly, especially during tornado season.
  5. Secure Your Home: Reinforce your home, especially roofs and doors. Secure outdoor objects that could become projectiles.
  6. Identify Safe Places: Know the safest place in your home to take shelter, such as a basement, storm cellar, or an interior room on the lowest floor without windows.
  7. Practice Drills: Conduct regular tornado drills with your family to ensure everyone knows what to do and where to go.
  8. Install NOAA Weather Radio: Have a NOAA weather radio with an alarm feature to receive alerts.

Don’ts:

  • Ignore Tornado Risks: Don’t assume you’re safe from tornadoes if they haven’t occurred in your area recently.
  • Leave Loose Objects Outside: Don’t leave outdoor furniture, tools, or toys unsecured as they can become dangerous projectiles.
  • Neglect Maintenance: Don’t ignore structural weaknesses in your home that could be reinforced.
  • Forget About Pets: Don’t forget to include pets in your emergency planning.

    • During a Tornado

    Do's:

    1. Seek Shelter Immediately: If a tornado warning is issued, take shelter immediately in a safe location.
    2. Use the Sturdiest Shelter Available: Go to a basement, storm cellar, or an interior room on the lowest floor away from windows and exterior walls.
    3. Protect Your Head and Neck: Use a mattress, heavy blankets, or a helmet to protect yourself from debris.
    4. Listen for Updates: Use a battery-powered weather radio or mobile app to receive updates and information.
    5. Cover Yourself: If possible, get under sturdy furniture and use your arms to protect your head and neck.
    6. Stay Indoors: Stay inside until the tornado has passed and it’s safe to come out.

    Don’ts:

    1. Don’t Stay in a Mobile Home: Mobile homes are not safe during a tornado. Seek sturdier shelter immediately.
    2. Avoid Windows: Don’t stand near windows or glass doors, as they can shatter.
    3. Don’t Try to Outrun a Tornado in a Vehicle: It’s safer to abandon your vehicle and seek shelter in a sturdy building or a low-lying area.
    4. Don’t Go Outside: Don’t leave your shelter until you are sure the tornado has passed and it is safe to do so.

    After a Tornado

    Do's:

    1. Check for Injuries: Check yourself and others for injuries. Administer first aid if necessary and call for emergency medical help if needed.
    2. Inspect Your Home: Check your home for structural damage before re-entering. Be cautious of downed power lines, broken glass, and other hazards.
    3. Listen for Updates: Continue to monitor local news and weather updates for information on further storms and safety instructions.
    4. Document Damage: Take photos and videos of the damage for insurance purposes.
    5. Stay Safe: Be aware of potential hazards such as gas leaks, electrical hazards, and structural damage.
    6. Follow Official Instructions: Follow the instructions of local authorities regarding safety and recovery efforts.

    Don’ts:

    1. Don’t Use Matches or Lighters: Avoid using open flames until you are sure there are no gas leaks.
    2. Avoid Downed Power Lines: Don’t touch downed power lines or objects in contact with them.
    3. Don’t Enter Damaged Buildings: Avoid buildings that appear structurally unsound until they have been inspected by professionals.
    4. Avoid Driving: Don’t drive unless it is absolutely necessary, as roads may be blocked or damaged.
    5. Don’t Drink Tap Water Until Safe: Avoid drinking tap water until authorities have confirmed it is safe to do so.
