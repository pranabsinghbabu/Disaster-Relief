DO'S AND DONTS ON HURRICANE

Before a Hurricane

Do's:

  1. Know Your Risk:
    • Determine if you live in a hurricane-prone area by checking local hazard maps and resources.
    • Understand the difference between a hurricane watch (conditions possible) and a hurricane warning (conditions expected).
  2. Create an Emergency Plan:
    • Develop a family emergency plan, including evacuation routes, a communication plan, and a designated meeting place.
    • Ensure all family members know the plan and practice it regularly.
  3. Prepare an Emergency Kit:
    • Include water (one gallon per person per day for at least three days), non-perishable food, medications, flashlights, batteries, first aid supplies, important documents, and cash.
    • Don't forget supplies for pets, such as food, water, and medications.
  4. Secure Your Home:
    • Install storm shutters or board up windows with plywood.
    • Secure or bring indoors outdoor furniture, decorations, and other items that could become projectiles.
    • Reinforce garage doors to prevent them from being blown in.
    • Trim trees and shrubs to reduce the risk of branches causing damage.
  5. Stay Informed:
    • Sign up for local emergency alerts and monitor weather forecasts regularly.
    • Have a battery-powered NOAA weather radio for updates in case of power outages.
  6. Review Insurance Policies:
    • Ensure your homeowners or renters insurance covers hurricane damage, including wind and flood damage.
    • Take photos or videos of your property for insurance purposes.
  7. Plan for Power Outages:
    • Stock up on batteries, and consider purchasing a generator.
    • Charge all electronic devices before the storm hits.

Don’ts:

  1. Ignore Evacuation Orders:
    • Don’t stay behind if authorities order an evacuation. Your safety is paramount.
  2. Leave Loose Items Outside:
    • Don’t leave outdoor furniture, tools, or toys unsecured; they can become dangerous projectiles in high winds.
  3. Neglect Home Maintenance:
    • Don’t ignore structural weaknesses in your home that could be reinforced.
  4. Forget About Pets:
    • Don’t forget to include your pets in your emergency planning and evacuation plan.

    During a Hurricane

    Do's:

    1. Stay Indoors:
      • Stay inside and away from windows, skylights, and glass doors.
      • Go to a small, interior, windowless room on the lowest level of your home, such as a bathroom or closet.
    2. Monitor Updates:
      • Use a battery-powered radio to stay updated on the storm's progress and emergency information.
    3. Follow Official Instructions:
      • Listen to local authorities and follow their instructions regarding safety and evacuation.
    4. Keep Emergency Supplies Handy:
      • Ensure your emergency kit is within easy reach.
    5. Stay Calm:
      • Keep calm and ensure family members are safe and accounted for.

    Don’ts:

    1. Don’t Go Outside:
      • Don’t venture outside during the hurricane, even if it seems calm. The calm can be the eye of the storm, and dangerous conditions can resume abruptly.
    2. Avoid Using Candles:
      • Don’t use candles during a power outage due to the risk of fire. Use flashlights instead.
    3. Don’t Use Electrical Appliances:
      • Avoid using electrical appliances, especially if there is flooding.
    4. Don’t Open Windows:
      • Don’t open windows to relieve pressure; it’s a myth that this will prevent damage, and it can actually make things worse.

      After a Hurricane

      Do's:

      1. Wait for Official Clearance:
        • Return home only when authorities have declared it safe.
      2. Check for Injuries:
        • Administer first aid if necessary and seek medical help for serious injuries.
      3. Inspect Your Home:
        • Check your home for structural damage, gas leaks, and electrical hazards before entering.
        • Use a flashlight to inspect your home, not matches or candles.
      4. Document Damage:
        • Take photos and videos of the damage for insurance claims.
      5. Stay Informed:
        • Continue to monitor local news for updates on relief efforts and safety information.
      6. Clean Safely:
        • Wear protective clothing and masks while cleaning up debris.
        • Use proper safety gear when handling hazardous materials.
      7. Check Utilities:
        • Report downed power lines and gas leaks to authorities immediately.
        • Avoid using tap water until authorities confirm it is safe.

      Don’ts:

      1. Don’t Enter Floodwaters:
        • Avoid walking or driving through floodwaters, which can be contaminated or hide dangers.
      2. Don’t Use Contaminated Food or Water:
        • Discard any food, beverages, or medications that have been exposed to floodwaters or not stored properly.
      3. Avoid Using Electricity in Wet Areas:
        • Don’t use electrical appliances or turn on lights in areas with standing water.
      4. Don’t Overexert Yourself:
        • Avoid overexertion during the cleanup process; take breaks and stay hydrated.
